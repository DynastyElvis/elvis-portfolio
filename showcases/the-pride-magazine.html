The Pride Magazine

Crafting the Future of Pride Magazine

As the lead designer of Pride Magazine, I have played a pivotal role in shaping its visual identity, ensuring that every issue reflects the spirit and culture of the Kenya Airways family. From bold layouts to seamless typography, I have meticulously curated each edition to enhance readability and engagement. The magazine serves as a platform for employees to share stories, celebrate achievements, and stay informed about company developments, and my design approach ensures that this content is not only accessible but also visually compelling.

Bringing KQ’s Stories to Life

Through thoughtful design, I have transformed Pride Magazine into more than just an internal publication—it's a dynamic storytelling medium. Each section, from "Top KQ Moments" to "A Day in the Life of...", is designed to captivate readers with a balance of striking imagery and engaging content. My creative direction extends beyond aesthetics, incorporating interactive elements and layouts that encourage employees to connect with the brand and with each other. By blending innovation with tradition, I have helped make Pride Magazine a true reflection of KQ’s excellence and unity.

Designing with Precision - Tools, Collaboration, and Impact

  • Utilized Adobe InDesign, Photoshop, and Illustrator for high-quality layouts.
  • Collaborated with writers, photographers, and the internal comms team for cohesive storytelling.
  • Enhanced readability and engagement through modern design techniques.
  • Strengthened internal communication and employee connection to KQ’s brand.
